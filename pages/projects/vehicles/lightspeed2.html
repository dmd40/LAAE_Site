LIGHT SPEED II

Light Speed II was designed and constructed by the academy in 2000, 2 years after the first Light Speed. As a newer and improved model of its predecessor, Light Speed II went on to participate in the ZEV Challenge.

SPECIFICATIONS

Light Speed II was designed with hydraulic disk brakes and a Solectra DC Brush Type Permanent Magnet motor. LAAE constructed the vehicleâ€™s frame from carbon fiber and dual layer Nomex Honeycomb core material. Measuring 116 x 36 x 22 inches, Light Speed II recorded an average speed of 25-30 mph and a maximum speed of 40 mph.
